Second-harmonic generation (SHG) is a coherent, label-free optical signal produced specifically by non-centrosymmetric structures such as fibrillar collagen. I use SHG microscopy to image and quantify the three-dimensional organisation of collagen in connective tissues — skin, tendon, cornea, cartilage, vocal cords — and to relate this architecture to the mechanical behaviour of the tissue.

Combined with mechanical testing, SHG reveals how the collagen network reorganises under load and how it is remodelled by ageing or disease, turning an optical signal into a quantitative, multiscale readout of tissue biomechanics.
