Brillouin microscopy probes the mechanical properties of tissues all-optically and without contact, by analysing the GHz-frequency light scattered by thermal acoustic waves. I develop high-spectral-resolution Brillouin instruments — based on multi-stage VIPA spectrometers — to map the local stiffness of soft connective tissues such as vocal cords, cartilage and cornea.

The approach is combined with second-harmonic generation so that the architecture of the collagen network can be correlated with its biomechanics. This line of work is supported by the projects **HaBIm** (ANR JCJC) and **BriTis**, and by the PhD of Florestan Luga, in collaboration with F. Légaré (INRS-EMT) and A. Desoutter (Montpellier).
